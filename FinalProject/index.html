Meet the Interim Head Coach: Jeff Ulbrich

Jeff Ulbrich was named the interim head coach of the New York Jets in October 2024, stepping up to lead the team with a commitment to excellence and teamwork. A defensive mastermind, Ulbrich has brought intensity and strategy to the Jets' sideline.

Prior to his current role, Ulbrich served as the Jets' defensive coordinator, where he developed a formidable defense known for its discipline and toughness. He also spent time as the defensive coordinator for the San Francisco 49ers, contributing to their Super Bowl run.

As interim head coach, Jeff Ulbrich aims to instill a culture of resilience and accountability while guiding the Jets through the remainder of the season.

The History of the New York Jets

The New York Jets, founded in 1960 as the Titans of New York, are one of the most storied franchises in the NFL. In 1963, the team was renamed the Jets to reflect the city's excitement for air travel and its future home near LaGuardia Airport.

The Jets achieved their greatest triumph in 1969 when Joe Namath famously guaranteed and delivered a Super Bowl III victory over the Baltimore Colts. Since then, the team has remained a symbol of resilience and passion, with a fanbase that proudly bleeds green and white.

From iconic players like Joe Namath and Curtis Martin to modern stars like Aaron Rodgers and Garrett Wilson, the Jets continue to fight for glory on the gridiron.
